# Company Research

Knowledge module for analyzing companies, identifying culture patterns, and evaluating employers.

## Culture Analysis Patterns

### Startup Culture Indicators

**Early Stage (Seed - Series A)**
- Small team (< 50)
- Wear many hats
- High uncertainty, high equity
- Founder-led decision making
- Limited processes
- Fast iteration

**Growth Stage (Series B-D)**
- Scaling rapidly (50-500)
- Specialization emerging
- Process introduction
- Management layers forming
- Culture codification
- Growing pains common

**Late Stage / Pre-IPO**
- Large organization (500+)
- Established processes
- Political dynamics
- IPO preparation pressure
- Retention challenges
- Wealth events anticipated

### Enterprise Culture Indicators

**Positive Signs**
- Structured career ladders
- Comprehensive benefits
- Work-life balance emphasis
- Training programs
- Job stability
- Clear expectations

**Warning Signs**
- Bureaucratic slowness
- Limited innovation
- Legacy technology
- Siloed teams
- Change resistance
- Overly political

### Remote-First Indicators

**Genuine Remote-First**
- Distributed leadership
- Async communication emphasis
- Documentation culture
- Timezone awareness
- Home office stipends
- No HQ preference

**Remote-Tolerant (Not First)**
- Remote allowed but HQ preferred
- Real-time meeting heavy
- In-person events required
- Leadership centralized at HQ
- Career advancement concerns

## Red Flag Identification

### Organizational Red Flags

**High Turnover Signals**
- Same role posted repeatedly
- Many "new" employees on LinkedIn
- Reviews mention constant changes
- Glassdoor mentions "revolving door"
- Short average tenure (<2 years)

**Poor Management**
- Low manager ratings on Glassdoor
- Reviews cite micromanagement
- Lack of clear direction mentioned
- "Politics" frequently in reviews
- High exec turnover

**Unsustainable Expectations**
- Reviews mention long hours as norm
- "Startup pace" for mature company
- Burnout mentioned repeatedly
- PTO discouraged in practice
- After-hours expectations

### Financial Red Flags

**Funding Concerns**
- No recent funding for >18 months (private)
- Down round or flat round
- Layoffs despite "growth" messaging
- Quiet about metrics
- Competitors significantly better funded

**Public Company Concerns**
- Stock decline over 12+ months
- Analyst downgrades
- Earnings misses
- Executive departures
- Activist investors involved

### Interview Process Red Flags

**Disorganized Hiring**
- Excessive interview rounds (>6)
- Long delays between stages
- Interviewers unprepared
- Conflicting role descriptions
- No clear timeline

**Concerning Questions/Behavior**
- Illegal interview questions
- Negative talk about employees
- Pressure tactics on salary
- Unwilling to discuss compensation
- Vague about responsibilities

## Glassdoor/Review Interpretation

### Rating Context

**5.0 Rating**
- Suspiciously perfect
- May be curated/encouraged reviews
- Check review authenticity
- Look at negative reviews specifically

**4.0-4.5 Rating**
- Generally good employer
- Real reviews likely
- Read recent reviews for current state
- Check trends over time

**3.5-4.0 Rating**
- Mixed experiences
- Department variance likely
- Role-specific investigation needed
- Recent improvements possible

**3.0-3.5 Rating**
- Significant issues present
- Investigate specific concerns
- May be improving or declining
- Consider risk tolerance

**Below 3.0**
- Major problems likely
- Proceed with caution
- Verify if representative
- Ask directly in interviews

### Review Analysis

**Credibility Indicators**
- Specific examples given
- Balanced pros and cons
- Realistic language
- Verified employee status
- Reasonable tenure mentioned

**Suspicious Patterns**
- All 5-star in short period
- Identical language across reviews
- No cons listed
- Generic praise only
- Posted during PR crisis

### Trend Analysis

- Compare last 6 months to previous periods
- Note leadership changes
- Check for layoff timing correlation
- Look for culture shift indicators

## Industry-Specific Research

### Tech Companies

**Research Focus**
- Tech stack and modernization
- Engineering blog presence
- Open source contributions
- Conference participation
- Glassdoor engineering reviews specifically
- Levels.fyi for compensation

**Key Questions**
- What's the deployment frequency?
- How's the on-call culture?
- What's the tech debt situation?
- How autonomous are teams?

### Finance/Fintech

**Research Focus**
- Regulatory compliance burden
- Compliance team size
- Risk tolerance culture
- Compensation structure (base vs bonus)
- Work hour expectations

**Key Questions**
- What's the bonus structure?
- How regulated is the product?
- What's the compliance overhead?

### Healthcare/Biotech

**Research Focus**
- Clinical trial status (biotech)
- FDA approval pipeline
- R&D vs commercial balance
- Regulatory complexity
- Mission alignment

**Key Questions**
- What stage are products in?
- How stable is funding?
- What's the path to revenue?

### E-commerce/Retail

**Research Focus**
- Peak season expectations
- On-call requirements
- Customer support burden on engineering
- Speed vs quality culture
- Margin pressures

**Key Questions**
- What's Black Friday like?
- How are incidents handled?
- What's the uptime expectation?

## Research Source Quality

### High Reliability
- Official company statements
- SEC filings (public companies)
- Verified Glassdoor reviews
- LinkedIn data
- Press releases

### Medium Reliability
- News articles
- Industry reports
- Blind posts (anonymous but often accurate)
- Reddit discussions
- Conference talks

### Lower Reliability
- Anonymous comments
- Competitor statements
- Single reviews
- Social media posts
- Rumors

### Cross-Reference Strategy
1. Start with official sources
2. Validate with employee reviews
3. Check anonymous sources for patterns
4. Look for consistency across sources
5. Weight recent information higher
